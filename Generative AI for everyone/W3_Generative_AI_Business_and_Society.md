# Generative AI for Everyone

## Business and Society

This document summarizes business and societal implications of
Generative AI.

## 1. AI Automates Tasks, Not Jobs

Jobs consist of multiple tasks. AI automates specific tasks rather than
entire roles.

## 2. Augmentation vs Automation

Augmentation: AI assists humans. Automation: AI performs tasks fully.
Organizations often begin with augmentation.

## 3. Evaluating Automation Potential

Two factors: - Technical feasibility - Business value

## 4. Impact Across Sectors

Knowledge workers and higher-paid roles show greater AI exposure. Major
impacted functions include: - Sales - Marketing - Software engineering -
Legal - Finance

## 5. Societal Concerns

-   Bias amplification
-   Job displacement
-   Safety risks

Mitigation includes: - Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback
(RLHF) - Responsible AI practices

## 6. Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)

AGI refers to AI capable of performing any intellectual task a human
can. Currently aspirational.

## 7. Responsible AI Dimensions

-   Fairness
-   Transparency
-   Privacy
-   Security
-   Ethical use

## 8. Final Takeaway

Generative AI can increase productivity and solve large-scale problems,
but requires responsible deployment and governance.
